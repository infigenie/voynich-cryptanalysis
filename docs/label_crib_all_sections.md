## Three robust, manuscript-wide findings

1. **A consistent "label register."** Across every section the labels are `o-/ot-/ok-`-dominated and **`qo`-poor**, the mirror image of body text (`ch-/qo-`-dominated). Labels everywhere share a common morphological frame distinct from running text. (Robust; large effect; reproducible.)

2. **Labels are section-specific.** Cross-section label-vocabulary overlap is near zero (Jaccard 0.01–0.05 for every pair of sections). Pharma labels ≠ astro labels ≠ zodiac labels. Each domain carries its own label vocabulary — exactly what *meaningful* names would do (plant names in pharma, star-type names in astro). (Robust.)

3. **Labels are a separate naming layer, not recycled body words** — strongest in Pharma (only 19% of label-types also in same-section body) and Astro (11%). They are highly unique (85–97% hapax), most of all in Astro, the section of star charts. (Robust for Pharma/Astro; smaller samples for Herbal/Cosmo.)

## Interpretation (audit-disciplined)

The combined picture — a distinct, section-specific, highly-unique naming layer sharing a common particle-like onset — is **the structure a real name list would have** (domain-specific names, each unique, all carrying a common prefix such as an article). This is a *mild* point in favour of meaning existing, and it identifies the labels (especially Astro star-labels and Pharma plant-labels) as **the meaning-bearing target**.

**But it does not discriminate decisively, and no crib closes:**
- The same statistics (section-specific + unique + common frame) are reproduced by a procedural/constructed generator; "name-like" ≠ "names."
- The common `o-/ot-/ok-` onset is suggestive of a definite-article prefix (Arabic `al-`, Romance `il-/el-`) but matches no language cleanly, and the Arabic-article path (Crowe 2022) failed blind reproducibility.
- No external anchor: no star catalogue, plant-name list, or bilingual the labels demonstrably map onto.
- The only genuine calendar crib (month names by some zodiac wheels) is in a **later Latin hand**, not the Voynichese stream, and shows no recoverable alignment to the labels.

## Verdict

The exhaustive crib hunt yields the **same result in every section**: labels are a real, distinct, name-like naming layer — the right place to look, and weak evidence that meaning exists — but they yield **no readable plaintext** for want of an anchor. This constrains and directs future work (focus on Astro/Pharma labels; test article-prefix hypotheses against catalogues) without itself unlocking meaning.

## Image reverse-engineering status

All six illustration domains have been decomposed into recurring visual primitives (herbal: leaf/flower/impossible-root; balneological: nymph-poses/pools/tubes; zodiac: ring+central-animal+starred-figure template; cosmological: rim+text-rings+spokes+medallion; pharmaceutical: modular base+body+neck+lid jars). Each domain is **combinatorial** — assembled from ~18–22 reused primitives via repeat/slot operators — paralleling the text's slot grammar. Caveat (audit): this rests partly on vision-model judgement and "combinatorial" is weakly diagnostic; it shows systematic construction, not meaning.
