# Reverse-engineering the balneological & astronomical sections

Last updated: 2026-06-22

## Astronomical volvelles (f67–73)

**Layout (picture-level):** a **central celestial face** (sun or moon) emitting **radial rays/spokes**, each ray ending in a **star**, with a **ring of stars** and **circumferential rim text**. Smaller facing diagrams carry crosses, small discs, and figures. Structurally these are **star-dials / volvelle-style diagrams** — the disk-primitive grammar (centre medallion + spokes + star-ring + rim text) with stars as the repeated unit.

**Text (script-level):** label/circular/radial-heavy — 139 labels + 277 circular + 151 radial vs only 263 paragraph tokens. The **radial labels (one per star/spoke)** are the candidate **star names**: they are the **most unique in the manuscript (97% hapax)** and carry the label register (`o-/ok-/y-/ot-` onset). This is exactly the surface a named-star catalogue would have.

**Crib verdict:** the radial star-labels are the single best "name list" in the manuscript — but there is **no anchor to a known star catalogue** (the Arabic-star-name route, Crowe 2022, fails to reproduce), and the same statistics arise from procedural generation. No crib closes. Picture-level reading is secure (celestial dials); the labels stay unread.

## Balneological "plumbing" (f75–84)

**Layout (picture-level):** networks of **green pools/reservoirs connected by tubes/conduits**, with small **nude female figures ("nymphs")** standing inside the pools, embedded in dense surrounding text. The repeated primitives are pool/reservoir + tube-connector + nymph (see the combinatorial-imagery analysis).

**Competing iconographic interpretations (all speculative, none proven):**
- **Balneological** — bathing / health-spa / hydrotherapy scenes.
- **Anatomical / gynaecological** — the tubes and reservoirs as organs/vessels (reproductive system) and the nymphs as a treatise on generation.
- **Alchemical** — the pools and conduits as distillation/apparatus.

These are rival readings of the *pictures*; the manuscript supplies no internal confirmation of any.

**Text (script-level):** the section is **dense running Currier-B prose** — 6,725 paragraph tokens (67% hapax, *slightly more repetitive* than body, as Currier B is), top words `shedy ol chedy qokedy qokain` (canonical Currier B), plus 137 small figure-labels (88% hapax, label register). The prose is **statistically ordinary** — there is **no distinctive anatomical/technical vocabulary** that stands out as a labelled diagram-legend.

**Crib verdict:** ordinary Currier-B text with no special register; the competing anatomical/alchemical/balneological readings cannot be adjudicated from the text, which yields no crib.

## Pattern (consistent with the whole project)

Both sections repeat the project-wide result: **the iconography is legible** (star-dials; pool-and-tube networks) and even datable/contextualisable, while **the script is not** — labels are name-like but unanchored, and running text is ordinary Voynichese. Where a section is label-dominated (astronomical) the labels are maximally name-like (97% hapax); where it is prose-dominated (balneological) the text is maximally ordinary. Neither yields meaning.
