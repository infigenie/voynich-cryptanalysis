# Roadmap

Last updated: 2026-06-22

The internal-analysis programme is largely complete; remaining work is either (a) pressure-testing the cipher hypothesis or (b) honestly bounded, since reading the text needs external evidence.

## Near-term (still runnable internally)

- **Line-as-unit, quantified against Naibbe.** Obtain/generate Naibbe ciphertext and measure how much line-position structure it lacks vs the real manuscript (we measured the real residual at ~0.09 bits/word; the grammar gives ~0.001). Decides whether the line effect is fatal to current cipher models or fixable by nulls.
- **Deterministic CV-syllable respacing test.** Check whether Voynich word boundaries track Latin/Italian consonant-vowel syllables (Greshko's sketched fix).
- **Plaintext-language fingerprinting.** Test whether label/table-usage statistics fit Latin vs Italian phonotactics, to narrow the candidate plaintext if it is a cipher.

## Medium-term (scholarship-dependent)

- **Astronomical star-labels vs historical catalogues.** The 97%-hapax radial labels are the best name-list; systematically test against medieval star-name lists (beyond the failed Arabic route) — low expected yield, but the right place to look.
- **High-resolution imagery.** Re-run the rosettes/zodiac iconography on full-resolution Beinecke scans to verify cited features (Ghibelline merlons, causeways) that the current low-res facsimile cannot resolve.

## Out of scope / blocked

- **Reading the text** without a key, crib, or second document. Honest deferral, not a to-do.

## Verdict-changing events to watch for

Any of: a second manuscript in the script; a bilingual/crib; a recovered cipher key; or a decipherment that passes blind reproducibility. Absent these, the project's deliverable is the *characterisation and the bounded negative result*, not a reading.
